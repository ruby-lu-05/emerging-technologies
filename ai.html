What is Artificial Intelligence?

Artificial intelligence refers to the use of computers and machines to mimic the human mind's ability to solve problems and make decisions.

"Can machines think?"

- Alan Turing, 1950

There are four main approaches to AI:

  1. Thinking humanly
  2. Thinking rationally
  3. Acting humanly
  4. Acting rationally


Examples of Applications of Artificial Intelligence:

Speech Recognition

Customer Service

Natural Language Processing (NLP) can be used to convert human speech into a written form. Many mobile devices incorporate speech recognition to provide voice search (e.g Siri) or convenience when texting.

On e-commerce websites, virtual agents can replace human agents for answering FAQs, providing personalized advice, cross-selling products, suggesting sizes, and more.

Computer Vision

Recommendation Engines

Computer vision is when meaningful information is derived from digital images & videos. Based on the information derived, machines can take action. Examples of computer vision include photo tagging in social media, radiology imaging in healthcare, and self-driving cars.

Recommendation engines discover data trends that can be used to develop cross-selling strategies. They can make relevant recommendations for customers.


Types of Artificial Intelligence:

Narrow AI

These kinds of machines are trained to perform specific tasks (e.g checking weather, playing chess, analyzing data). They don't perform outside of the single task that they are designed and trained to perform. Compared to even the most basic human intelligence, Narrow AI has much more constraints and limitations. However, Narrow AI systems can still process data and complete their designated tasks significantly quicker and more efficiently than any human can.

Strong AI

Strong AI, which can also be categorized as General AI or Super AI, is a theoretical form of AI in which machines can exhibit the same or a higher level of intelligence as humans. Strong AI would be self-aware, sentient, and able to perform any intellectual task that humans can perfom. Currently, Strong AI is still theoretical and there are no practical examples in use today. AI researchers are exploring its development, but a human's sentient qualities are very difficult to replicate in machines.





Currently, the majority of AI that surrounds us can be categorized as Narrow AI (e.g Google search, image recognition, self-driving cars, Siri).
