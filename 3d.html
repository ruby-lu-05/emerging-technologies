What is 3D Printing?

3D printing is a method of manufacturing that involves using computer-aided design (CAD) to produce 3D objects with a variety of materials like molten plastic or powders. 3D printers use a layering method to create objects. In other words, they work from the ground up and add layers after layers until the object looks like the design.


3D printing can be used to
produce rigid objects.

It can also be used to produce flexible
objects using hybrid rubber.


How do 3D Printers Work?

1) 3D Modelling

The first step of the 3D printing process is always 3D modelling. Objects need to be designed in a 3D modelling software in order to maximize precision and let the 3D printer know what needs to be done.


2) Slicing

In order for the printer to produce the object, the design needs to be “sliced” into layers. Slicing software can scan each layer of the model and tell the printer how to recreate that layer.


3) Printing Process

3D printers have a nozzle that moves back and forth to dispense a wax or plastic-like polymer layer by layer. To create a 3D object, the printer essentially needs to add hundreds or thousands of 2D prints on top of each other.





Benefits of 3D Printing:

  • 3D printing can be extremely precise, since 3D modelling allows products to be customized down to the smallest details. For example, in dentistry, 3D software can be used to design custom teeth alignment tools that precisely fit the individual.

  • 3D printing can be used for rapid prototyping. Many companies can now use 3D printers to create prototypes in a matter of hours. It is estimated that 3D printers can make the prototyping process 10 times faster and 5 times cheaper.

  • There is more that can go wrong with traditional manufacturing because of human error and potential problems with machinery.

Price of 3D Printing:

The cost of 3D printers can vastly differ based on the size, specialty, or use. 3D printers can be as cheap as $200, but large industrial 3D printers can cost as much as $100,000. An average consumer would pay roughly $650 for a 3D printer. In the future, prices are expected to significantly decrease as a result of 3D printing technology becoming more widely-adopted and widespread.


Examples of Applications of 3D Printing:

Bioprinting

Construction

3D printers can use biomaterials to create replicas of natural structures and tissues within the human body. Medical devices like prosthetics can also be 3D printed.

3D printers are capable of building walls and processing cement. They can sometimes help create buildings and construction components more intricately than traditional manufacturing.


Aerospace Technology

Art & Jewelry

In the aerospace industry, 3D printing can often provide a way to reduce the number of parts, reduce weight, and increase strength.

3D printing allows for unique or customized pieces to be produced faster and at a lower cost. With 3D printing, jewelry makers can also experiment with designs that would be very difficult for traditional jewelry making methods to create.
